RESPONSE:
 Speaker: S Hargreaves, Councillor Obi, Reuben Marsh, Dr. Vasquez
Quote:
- S Hargreaves: "So I can start because I have been on deadline with the operational side of this for seven years now, we are currently retaining data for thirty one days. In practice though, by the time a victim reports something, and we get the request through to council, we are already at day twenty-eight, so we lose footage constantly." (Value: Public safety)
- Councillor Obi: "I hear you but I want to be careful that we do not let a handful of cases drive a policy that affects millions of people who have done nothing wrong." (Value: Privacy)
- Reuben Marsh: "Can I ask a question? Who is actually paying for extended storage? Because we are already over budget on the camera maintenance contract and if we are talking about doubling the retention time, then we should take into account the costs of that." (Value: Fiscal responsibility)
- Dr. Vasquez: "I think the cost question is quite important, I agree. As well as the cost of retention, we should take into account the privacy expectations when footage is retained and processed longer than normal. This is a human rights question." (Value: Privacy)
- Councillor Obi: "Right, I agree with you. Extending retention means that anyone who walked past a camera three months ago is still in the system. They didn’t consent to that, they aren’t a suspect." (Value: Privacy)
- D Hargreaves: "Nobody consents to CCTV either though, that ship has sailed. We have over six hundred cameras in this city alone." (Value: Public safety)
- Dr. Vasquez: "I think there is a difference worth drawing out though. Passive retention of footage is one thing, but if the footage is being automatically processed or analysed, that is a concern to bring up." (Value Tension: Privacy vs. Public safety)
- D Hargreaves: "At the moment there isn’t. This is purely manual review on request." (Note: Clarifying that there is no automatic processing or analysis of footage at this time)
- Reuben Marsh: "So practically speaking, if we are going to change the retention window, what are the options? Ninety days? Six months? Or is there some sort of standard?" (Value: Fiscal responsibility)
- Dr. Vasquez: "Scotland has moved to ninety days for certain camera types with tiered oversight, so there is a model." (Note: Providing an example of another country's policy as a potential solution)
- D Hargreaves: "Mandatory deletion auditing is a good idea actually, it would help us too because right now we have no visibility on whether cameras are even recording properly." (Value: Public safety)
- Reuben Marsh: "Agreed, that seems like a reasonable starting point. Though I’ll say again, someone needs to check the costs of all this before making commitments." (Value: Fiscal responsibility)
- Dr. Vasquez: "The other thing I would want to see is a clear purpose limitation written into any new policy. Like people should have to justify retrieval of footage beyond thirty one days, otherwise you extend the window and the scope quietly expands over time." (Value: Privacy)
- D Hargreaves: "I would support that too." (Note: Endorsing Dr. Vasquez's suggestion)
- Councillor Obi: "I agree with that as well but I am still not convinced on the number of days. I think a tiered approach based on offence type is at least a framework worth exploring." (Value: Fiscal responsibility and public safety)
- Reuben Marsh: "I think, as we said before, we can use examples of other countries that have successfully implemented responsible extension of footage retention in our approach." (Note: Endorsing the idea of using other countries' policies as a guide)
- Councillor Obi: "How are we going to quantify success in those cases?" (Value: Fiscal responsibility and public safety)
- Dr. Vasquez: "I think running a pilot program of our own and crunching the numbers on useful approaches might be better for our case as well instead of blindly following other countries." (Note: Proposing a pilot program to test different retention policies)
- Councillor Obi: "Okay, but the pilot strategy still doesn’t tackle the core issue for me, those people in the footage are still unaware that their data is being kept for longer than normal." (Value: Privacy)
- D Hargreaves: "I would be the first to think about people’s privacy, but we also have a duty to keep everyone safe. I think having a pilot program is a fair compromise." (Note: Offering a compromise between public safety and privacy concerns)
- Councillor Obi: "I agree, if you want to do a pilot program, informing and getting approval of the residents in the area is important." (Note: Endorsing the idea of informing residents about the pilot program)

Construct Type: Value, Value Tension, Consensus Point
Label: Public safety, Privacy, Fiscal responsibility
Notes: The values of public safety, privacy, and fiscal responsibility are all expressed by multiple speakers throughout the discussion. A value tension arises between privacy and public safety when Dr. Vasquez brings up the concern of automatic processing or analysis of footage. There is a consensus point on the idea of informing residents about any changes to CCTV retention policies and running a pilot program to test different approaches.
