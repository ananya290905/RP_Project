RESPONSE:
 Speaker: Director Font, Amara Diea, Dr. Hoof, Councillor Lemaire, Fatima Rashid

Quote:
- Director Font: "I will be direct with the intentions of this, we retain biometric data collected at entry points for five years." (Value: Data retention)
- Amara Diea: "You have tourists, students, refugees coming in and treating all of them as a potential security risk by default and holding their data is not a neutral administrative decision." (Value: Privacy, fairness)
- Dr. Hoof: "Can I ask what the actual security justification is for five years specifically?" (Value: Security)
- Director Font: "Investigations take time, you might not know someone is relevant to an inquiry until two or three years after they entered." (Value: Investigation efficiency)
- Councillor Lemaire: "But I think it is also the kind of exceptional case that tends to drive policy in ways that affect millions or ordinary people who never become relevant to any investigation." (Value: Fairness, proportionality)
- Amara Diea: "And it is worth saying who bears the cost of that infrastructure disproportionately." (Value: Equity)
- Director Font: "That is an implementation question not a retention question." (Value: Separation of concerns)
- Amara Diea: "I don’t think you can separate them that cleanly. The existence of the database enables the bias." (Value Tension: Data retention vs. fairness, privacy)
- Dr. Hoof: "There is also a question of what happens when this data is breached." (Value: Security, privacy)
- Director Font: "Every system carries breach risk. The question is whether the security benefit outweighs it and for me, it does." (Value: Risk management)
- Councillor Lemaire: "I think most of us would accept that some retention is justified. The disagreement is about duration, scope and access." (Value: Security, fairness)
- Dr. Hoof: "If the purpose is border security then border agencies having access during the period of a person’s visa or leave is defensible. Domestic law enforcement access under warrant only is a different proposition to what’s on the table." (Value Tension: Border security vs. privacy, oversight)
- Fatima Rashid: "For someone who has fled an authoritarian government, having their biometric data in a state database that could be accessed, potentially shared with other states, is not an abstract concern. It is a life or death question for some people." (Value: Safety, privacy)
- Amara Diea: "Yes, exactly. And the data sharing agreements we currently have with countries are not public, so we do not actually know where this data ends up." (Value Tension: Privacy vs. transparency)
- Director Font: "Data sharing is governed by strict bilateral agreements and legal frameworks. I understand the concern but it is not as if we are sending this to anyone who asks." (Value: Legality, accountability)
- Fatima Rashid: "With respect, the legal frameworks do not always hold. There have been cases of data being shared outside agreed channels." (Value Tension: Legality vs. accountability)
- Councillor Lemaire: "I think that is a fair point and it is one of the reasons I would want any expanded retention to come with an independent audit mechanism." (Value: Accountability, oversight)
- Director Font: "I am not opposed to oversight in principle. My concern is that every additional layer of process slows down the use of the data when speed matters." (Value Tension: Oversight vs. efficiency)
- Dr. Hoof: "That tension between oversights and speed is valid but I still don’t think it resolves in favour of less oversight just because it is inconvenient." (Value: Accountability, oversight)
- Fatima Rashid: "Can I ask whether there has been any consultation with the communities most affected by this?" (Value: Inclusion, representation)
- Amara Diea: "I agree, if we are deciding what is acceptable and what is not, we should consult the people who are most affected." (Value: Inclusion, representation)

Construct Type: Value, Value Tension, Consensus Point

Label: Data retention, privacy, fairness, security, risk management, accountability, oversight, transparency, inclusion, representation

Notes: The values expressed in this deliberative transcript revolve around data retention, privacy, fairness, security, and accountability. There are several instances of value tensions, such as the tension between data retention and fairness/privacy, oversight and efficiency, and legality and accountability. A consensus point is that some form of consultation with affected communities should take place when deciding on expanded data retention policies.
